Suicide Cases Around the World

The choropleth map on the right shows the normalized suicide cases per 100,000 population around the world. Darker shades of color represent a higher suicide rate per 100,000 population in that particular country and from that map, we can see that Russia and Lithuania are having the highest suicide rate per 100,000 population as compared to other countries due to their obvious darker shade of color.

One of the factors that caused Russia to have such a high suicide rate is its enormous population of citizens.

There are certain countries colored grey due to limited data.


Suicide Cases Based on Age

The line chart on the left shows the total suicide cases for different ages over the years.

From there, we can see that overall, people with age between 35-54 years have the highest number of suicide cases throughout the year 1985 to 2015. I assumed that the reason for this might be due to them facing huge financial difficulties which makes them give up on their life to avoid paying the debt.

On the other hand, people with age between 5-14 years have the lowest number of suicide cases throughout the year 1985 to 2015 as compared to other ages. This does make sense because people with age between 5-14 years does not suffer from any mental health issues as well as financial issues.


Suicide Cases Based on Gender

The stacked bar chart on the right shows the total suicide cases between males and females over the years.

From there, we can see that overall, the number of cases for males is more than for females over the years.

Based on research, although females attempt suicide at a higher rate as compared to males, they are more likely to use methods that are less lethal such as drug overdose and if they are noticed in the early stage by others, then they will have a chance to survive.


Percentage of Suicide Cases in Different Regions

The waffle chart on the left shows the percentage of suicide cases in different regions.

Based on the chart, the Asia region has the highest number of suicide cases with a total percentage of 40% followed by the Europe region with a total percentage of 33%.

The least percentage of suicide cases in the chart will be for the region, Oceania with a percentage of only 1%. Therefore, we can see that the people in the Oceania region are more stress-free as compared to other regions.


Top 10 Countries with the Highest Suicide Rate

The lollipop chart on the right shows the top 10 countries with the highest suicide cases from the year 1990 to 2014.

From the chart, we can see that country, Russia placed at the number one spot of having the highest number of suicide cases from the year 1990 to 2009.

However, after 2009, the United States of America overtook the spot from Russia to be the country with the highest number of suicide cases.

The United Kingdom is continuously placed in the last spot after the year 1994 as the country with the lowest suicide cases among the top 10 countries.


Percentage of Suicide Cases in Different Generations

The donut chart on the left shows the percentage of suicide cases in different generations.

Generation Boomers contribute 33.85% of the total number of suicide cases which is the highest compared to the other generations. Following it is the Silent Generation which contributes 26.40% and Generation X which contributes 22.71% of the total.

Generation Z contributes to the least percentage of suicide cases with only 0.24% of the total.
